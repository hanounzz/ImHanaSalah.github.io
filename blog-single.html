My WIE Journey

My journey to founding the IEEE WIE branch is one of my favourite personal milestones. It all began with planning and dreaming, and now we have a community of 70 active members who are all committed to the same goal: empowering women. Throughout my life, I have passionately advocated for women's rights, challenged stereotypes, and strongly opposed any gender-based violence. Unfortunately, this advocacy in my community was somehow misunderstood, portraying me as undermining men. However, my vision aligns seamlessly with one of WIE IEEE's core goals: career equality. I ardently believe in equal job opportunities for both men and women in STEM fields, where qualifications and skills take precedence over gender-related biases.

My journey with the IEEE WIE O6U branch has been a big step towards realising these aspirations. Providing a platform for breaking stereotypes and fostering a positive environment. The WIE community is more than women supporting women; it's about building a collaborative community where everyone supports each other in breaking barriers and advocating for a world where women are encouraged and celebrated for succeeding in any STEM endeavour.
